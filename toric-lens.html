Toric Lens

A Toric lens is a specialized intraocular lens (IOL) or contact lens designed to correct astigmatism.

What is a Toric Lens?

Toric lenses are special lenses designed to correct astigmatism, a condition where the cornea is irregularly shaped, causing blurry or distorted vision.These lenses come in both contact lens form and as intraocular lenses (IOLs) used in cataract surgery.


How Toric Lenses Work:

Toric lenses have different powers in different meridians, addressing both spherical and cylindrical vision problems caused by astigmatism.The design ensures that the lens stays in place on the eye, providing consistent and clear vision.

Types of Toric Lenses:

Toric Contact Lenses: These are designed to be worn on the eye's surface and are available in both soft and rigid gas permeable materials.

Toric Intraocular Lenses (IOLs): These lenses are implanted during cataract surgery and are designed to correct both cataracts and astigmatism.

Benefits of Toric Lenses:

Clearer Vision:Offers sharp, focused vision for people with astigmatism.

Comfortable:Toric contact lenses fit snugly on the eye, ensuring that they don’t rotate and cause vision disturbances.

Versatile:Available for both contact lens wearers and those undergoing cataract surgery.

Who Should Use Toric Lenses?

People with astigmatism who need clear, precise vision correction.

Individuals undergoing cataract surgery who want a solution to correct both the cataract and astigmatism in one procedure.

Those who prefer contact lenses but suffer from astigmatism.
